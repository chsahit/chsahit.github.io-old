Neural Network Optimizer

The Agency at the GT College of Computing

I built this my first semester of freshman year with a few other students as an introduction to classical Artificial Intelligence as well as more modern machine learning. The problem this project aims to solve is that typically the "learning rate" of a neural network has to be tuned by hand. As is the case with hand tuned parameters, the learning rate takes time to tune and it is painful to do it methodically. Our solution was to use a genetic algorithm to optimize the neural network. The deign and implementation of this genetic algorithm was my contribution to this project. As a opposed to a randomly selected learning rate, the genetic algorithm was able to calculate one that lead to a 20% gain in accuracy. Click the link for the github!

FIRST Stronghold: Platypus

Mount Olive Robotics Team

This was my High School FIRST Robotics team's creation after 6 weeks to design, build, and program. Our challenge involved being able to cross uneven terrain and launch foam balls into the tower. As lead developer I wrote the motion profiling and PID controllers that enabled our robot to navigate autonomously into the tower. I also used a combination of feedback and feedforward control to spin the flywheel up to our shooting RPM. Keeping the back intake level was particularly challenging as the robot had to deal with a constantly changing setpoint given by the drivers as well as all the jostling from contact with other robots. As a result, I had to learn how to methodically tune PID controllers that I also wrote. Later in the season the robot was able to use its camera because of the work teammates and I did with OpenCV do get the robot to identify the target. Our robot seeded first and then lead the winning team at the Springside-Chestnut Hill district competition.
