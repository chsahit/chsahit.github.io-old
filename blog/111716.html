Why Finishing a Project is so hard

At any point in time I have a number of individual projects going on. I'm trying to do some image processing on my FPGA, play with a genetic algorithm that optimizes neural networks for AI club, learn scala and implement A* with it, and write a kalman filter for RoboJackets. There are also hobbies I want to pursue like tennis and piano. While I may finish the club projects I do, I always struggle to finish my personal ones. There are two things to consider with this: why do I struggle to finish personal projects and is it really a bad thing?

The answer to that first question is relatively trivial. Time is a valuable resource. It's simply more practical to allocate my time towards school work and group projects where I am acountable to someone other than myself. I owe it to myself to get the work assigned to me done and I owe it to teammates to finish the work I commited to doing.

However, that's not the entire story. I discussed this in my first post as well, but my time management is not where it could be. Sure I get done what I need to get done, but there's so much more to do. I think if I sacrifice another hour and a half of my day to sleep I would be far more productive with my remaining ~16 hours. I should be able to test that tonight.

Now to the question of whether it's really so bad that I fail to personal projects. The answer that: It depends. Usually, I set out on a project to learn something new. If I finish enough of the project to learn what I wanted to learn then that's great. For instance, I would love if my implementation of A* came with a decent GUI to illustrate what it was doing. However, not having such GUI really wouldn't add to my understanding of search algorithms. The exception to this, is me setting out to build a product that's meant to be used by people. In that instance, aesthetic and user interface are just as important as core functionality
