The Hamilton Post!

I just finished watching the documentary "Hamilton's America", and man, what a ride. I also spent a chunk of today at Ronald Reagan's Presidential library, so today's a day I'm proud to be an American. I think the takeaway from the two experiences was that leaders who are so often hyped up are at the end of the day, human beings. They are flawed like the rest of us but they were just trying to do what's right. I think everyone whishes that someone with Reagan's demeanor represented GOP in our general election. The museum was really well put together for the most part and it was interesting seeing how Reagan grew from a presidential boy to a presidential man.

Back to Hamilton. I am O B S S E S S E D. The musical itself, the mixtape,, and the documentary were all incredibly well put together and captivating. The musical transitions in the musical made listening to the whole play consecutively absolutely worth. I also liked that we saw the flaws of the protagonist, Alexander Hamilton, and the the virtues of the antagonist, Aaron Burr. I was able to relate to aspects of both characters. Seeing Ben Folds and Sia both appear on the mixtape was awesome and I was really excited to hear more from Lin Manuel Miranda as well. If there's one thing the Hamilton line has demonstrated, it's that LMM is a genius. But it goes to show how important it is to read books and to draw inspiration from the things in your life around you. I walked away from listening to the musical with an appreciation of America that I couldn't get from reading a textbook. As Miranda said so well in his documentary, reading about Hamilton's vigor really makes you question what you're doing in your own life.
