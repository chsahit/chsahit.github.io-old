Winging it I guess

I straight up have no clue what I'm going to write about

Yeah, not even a little!

Its finals week! I have exams in linear and psych as well as a portfolio I need to write for english. Linear was Thursday and went fairly well, but in retrospect I could have proved things a little better. I'm writing an SVM for pixel classification for robojackets and the math is pretty cool. It's like the least squares lines we learned how to generate in linear but now we're trying to maximize distances between two classes of data points. Maybe when I get the code ported to C++ I'll write about it.

I'm currently reading up on social psychology in preparation for my upcoming final. Part of me wants to think of social psych as a case study in why people are awful, but that's not really fair. Turns out it's pretty easy to get people to work together... in theory. Reading the chapter enforces the fact that people aren't fundamentally "bad"/racist/*ist. Typically they just lack exposure to people who aren't like them. Even if travel is too expensive, the advancement of technology makes it pretty easy to join a community of people who you disagree with. Turns out all humans have something in common, even if we don't like to limit. Group Polarization is also a very real problem where people tend to radicalize, alienating them from the values that others hold. It is this polarization that is likely the cause of our current political client and why it's important to make friends with people who disagree with you. There are ways to prevent group Polarization, such as encouraging dissent, and having a dedicated "Devil's Advocate".
