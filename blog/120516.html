Flight

Suspended in cold, unforgiving air
Bathed in warmth, book in hand, music in ear
Brisk winds reflected by the metal's glare
If man wasn't meant to fly, why I am I here?
Others are silent, save a loud baby's tears
The lights are dim amongst the lack of din
I've been up here before, so no more fear
People are crammed in their seats, bags in the bins
Against the starry backdrop the plane's fin
Man's tendrils invading nature's dark canvas
Beneath me a network of lights, lines thin
Evidence of growth so mind-numbingly fast
Spending a few hours completely alone
So I can be finally home
